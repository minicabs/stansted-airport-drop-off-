Stansted Airport Drop Off Charge Guide

Everything you need to know about the £7 drop-off fee at London Stansted Airport—how to pay, avoid fines, and plan your journey smoothly in 2026.

Current Drop-Off Fee at Stansted Airport

As of February 2026, all vehicles using the **designated drop-off zone** directly outside the terminal at London Stansted Airport must pay a **£7 charge**. This fee applies to every entry into the forecourt area, regardless of how long you stay.

The charge was introduced by Manchester Airports Group (MAG), which operates Stansted, to reduce congestion, improve air quality, and encourage use of the free alternative options.

  • Fee amount: £7 per vehicle per entry
  • When it applies: 24 hours a day, 365 days a year
  • Enforcement: Automatic Number Plate Recognition (ANPR) cameras
  • Penalty for non-payment: Up to £100 (reduced to £50 if paid within 14 days)

How to Pay the Stansted Drop-Off Charge

Payment must be made **within 48 hours** of your drop-off. You can pay:

  • Online: Visit the official Stansted Airport drop-off payment page
  • By phone: Call 0330 333 0111 (standard UK rates apply)
  • At the airport: Payment kiosks are available in the terminal arrivals area

You’ll need your vehicle registration number and the date/time of your visit. Keep your payment confirmation email or reference number in case of disputes.

Key Rules for Drop-Off at Stansted

To ensure smooth operations and avoid penalties, follow these essential rules:

  • Only stop in the **official drop-off zone** (forecourt) if you’ve paid the £7 fee.
  • No waiting or lingering—even for 30 seconds—without payment.
  • Do not double-park or block traffic flow.
  • Drivers must remain with the vehicle at all times during drop-off.
  • Unpaid drop-offs trigger automatic Penalty Charge Notices (PCNs) via ANPR.

Note: The rule applies to all private vehicles, including minicabs, ride-shares (Uber, Bolt), and personal cars. Only licensed taxis have different arrangements (see exemptions).

Who Is Exempt from the Charge?

Very few exemptions exist. The following may qualify:

  • Blue Badge holders: Must pre-register online and display the badge visibly. Still subject to time limits.
  • Pre-booked assistance passengers: If you’ve arranged special assistance with the airline, contact Stansted customer service for guidance.
  • Licensed London black cabs: Traditional taxis with PHV (Public Hire Vehicle) licenses may have separate access—but **minicabs do NOT qualify** unless under specific contracts.

Private hire vehicles (minicabs) are **not exempt**, even if licensed by local councils. Always assume you must pay unless officially confirmed otherwise.

Smart Alternatives to Avoid the £7 Fee

Savvy travelers and drivers use these free options:

  • Use the free Set-Down Area: Located just a 5-minute walk from the terminal via covered walkway. Completely free, well-lit, and monitored.
  • Book a meet-and-greet service: Many minicab firms include terminal drop-off in their fare—confirm if they’ve paid the charge on your behalf.
  • Use public transport: Stansted Express trains run frequently from London Liverpool Street; buses also serve the airport.
  • Plan ahead: Allow extra time if using the free drop-off zone, especially during peak travel hours.

For frequent users (e.g., airport workers or regular chauffeurs), consider setting up an account with Stansted’s payment system for faster processing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a free drop-off point at Stansted?

Yes! The free Set-Down area is located near the mid-stay car park, a short walk from the terminal. Follow signs for “Free Drop-Off.”

Do I pay if I’m just picking someone up?

No—the £7 charge applies only to **drop-offs** in the forecourt. Pick-ups use the free Short Stay car park (first 15 minutes free).

Can I get a refund if I paid but didn’t drop off?

Refunds are rarely granted. Payment is for entry into the zone, not completion of drop-off. Double-check before paying.

How does this affect minicab drivers?

Minicab drivers must pay the £7 fee if dropping off at the terminal. Many include this in their fare—always clarify with your driver or booking company.
